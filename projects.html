Projects Globally Implemented

REDD+ (Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation)

REDD+ (Reducing Emissions from Deforestation and Forest Degradation) is a global initiative launched in 2005 and formally adopted in 2010 under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC). Its aim is to incentivize developing countries to reduce emissions from deforestation and forest degradation while promoting sustainable forest management, conservation of forest carbon stocks, and reforestation. By assigning a financial value to the carbon stored in forests, REDD+ encourages countries to protect their forests and enhance carbon sequestration. This initiative not only helps mitigate climate change but also supports biodiversity conservation and the livelihoods of local communities, particularly indigenous peoples. Through capacity building, policy development, and result-based financial rewards, REDD+ fosters a balanced approach to forest conservation that aligns environmental goals with economic development.

Trillion Trees Initiative

The Trillion Trees Initiative is a global environmental movement launched with the goal of conserving, restoring, and planting one trillion trees worldwide by 2030. The initiative is backed by major organizations like the World Wildlife Fund (WWF), the Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS), and the European Union, among others, and aims to combat climate change by significantly increasing the Earth's tree cover. Trees play a crucial role in capturing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, thus mitigating global warming. The Trillion Trees Initiative focuses on three key strategies: protecting existing forests, restoring degraded lands, and planting new forests. By working with governments, local communities, businesses, and other stakeholders, the initiative seeks to scale up global reforestation efforts and ensure long-term forest conservation. This ambitious project is aligned with global climate goals, such as the Paris Agreement, and aims to increase biodiversity, protect ecosystems, and provide economic and social benefits to communities, especially in rural and forest-dependent regions. Through this collaborative effort, the initiative hopes to restore ecosystems, create jobs, and contribute to a sustainable and resilient future for the planet.
