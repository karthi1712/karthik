Forest's In India

Tamil Nadu & Kerala

  • Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve Location: Nilgiri district, and parts extend into Karnataka and Kerala. Description: A UNESCO biosphere reserve known for its unique biodiversity.

  • National Park Location: Nilgiri district. Description: Part of the Nilgiri Biosphere, famous for tigers, elephants, and other wildlife.

  • Sathyamangalam Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Erode district. Description: Largest wildlife sanctuary in Tamil Nadu, important habitat for elephants and tigers.

  • Tiger Reserve Location: Coimbatore and Tiruppur districts. Description: Known for its scenic forests and diverse wildlife, including elephants and tigers.

  • Kalakkad Mundanthurai Tiger Reserve Location: Tirunelveli and Kanyakumari districts. Description: Dense tropical forest known for tigers, leopards, and Indian bison.

  • Gulf of Mannar Marine National Park Location: Coast of Tamil Nadu, near Rameswaram. Description: Includes coastal forests, mangroves, and coral reefs, crucial for marine biodiversity.

  • Meenakshi Forest Reserve Location: Near Madurai. Description: Known for its rich birdlife and traditional uses by local communities.

  • Javadhu Hills Forest Location: Tiruvannamalai and Vellore districts. Description: Rich in medicinal plants and is culturally significant.

  • Shevaroy Hills Location: Salem district. Description: Known for coffee plantations and thick forests.

  • Srivilliputhur Grizzled Squirrel Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Virudhunagar and Madurai districts. Description: Important habitat for the endangered grizzled giant squirrel.
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Idukki district. Description: Famous for elephants, tigers, and scenic views around Periyar Lake.

  • Silent Valley National Park Location: Palakkad district. Description: Pristine rainforest with unique flora and fauna.

  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Wayanad district. Description: Part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve, rich in wildlife diversity.

  • Parambikulam Tiger Reserve Location: Palakkad district, near the Kerala-Tamil Nadu border. Description: Known for tigers, leopards, and other wildlife, with well-preserved forests.

  • Eravikulam National Park Location: Idukki district. Description: Grasslands and shola forests, home to the endangered Nilgiri tahr.

  • Shendurney Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Kollam district. Description: Tropical forests with diverse plant and animal life.

  • Peppara Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Thiruvananthapuram district. Description: Known for its rich biodiversity and the Peppara Dam.

  • Idukki Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Idukki district. Description: Surrounded by rivers and famous for elephants and birdlife.

  • Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Idukki district. Description: Known for dry forests and unique species like the grizzled giant squirrel.

  • Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Thiruvananthapuram district. Description: Tropical forest with Neyyar Dam, home to elephants, leopards, and more.

Karnataka & Andhra Pradesh

  • Bandipur National Park Location: Chamarajanagar district. Description: Part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve; home to tigers, elephants, and more.

  • Nagarhole National Park (Rajiv Gandhi National Park) Location: Mysore and Kodagu districts. Description: Known for dense forests and a rich variety of wildlife.

  • Bhadra Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Chikkamagaluru and Shivamogga districts. Description: Famous for tigers, leopards, and riverine ecosystems.

  • National Park Location: Chikkamagaluru district. Description: Hilly landscapes with shola forests, home to lion-tailed macaques.

  • Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Kodagu district. Description: Known for evergreen forests and rich biodiversity.

  • Biligiriranga Hills (BR Hills) Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Border of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Description: Hosts elephants, tigers, and unique ecosystems.

  • Valley Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Shivamogga district. Description: Lush valleys near the Sharavathi River, known for scenic landscapes.

  • Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Uttara Kannada district. Description: A well-known tiger and elephant reserve with dense forests and rivers.

  • Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Udupi district. Description: Part of the Western Ghats; home to rich bird species and dense flora.

  • Bannerghatta National Park Location: Near Bengaluru. Description: Offers a mix of forested areas and a zoo, home to elephants and leopards.

  • Sri Venkateswara National Park Location: Tirupati district. Description: Known for scenic landscapes and rich biodiversity.

  • Nallamala Forest Location: Extends across Kurnool, Prakasam, and Guntur districts. Description: Part of the Eastern Ghats, home to tigers, leopards, and tribal communities.

  • Papikonda National Park Location: East and West Godavari districts. Description: Dense forests along the Godavari River, rich in wildlife.

  • Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Visakhapatnam district. Description: Known for dry deciduous forests and varied fauna.

  • Rollapadu Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Kurnool district. Description: Famous for the Great Indian Bustard and grassland ecosystems.

  • Gundla Brahmeswaram Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Prakasam and Kurnool districts. Description: Dense forests that provide habitats for tigers and other species.

  • Maredumilli Forest Location: East Godavari district. Description: Known for lush greenery and waterfalls, popular for eco-tourism.

  • Coringa Wildlife Sanctuary Location: East Godavari district. Description: Mangrove forest near the coast, with diverse birdlife.

  • Seshachalam Hills Location: Chittoor and Kadapa districts. Description: Known for Red Sanders trees and unique flora.

  • Krishna Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Krishna district. Description: Coastal forest area, important for mangroves and birdlife.

Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh & Uttarakhand

  • Dachigam National Park Location: Near Srinagar. Description: Known for the endangered Hangul deer and diverse landscapes from grasslands to alpine meadows.

  • Kishtwar High Altitude National Park Location: Kishtwar district. Description: High-altitude park known for snow leopards, ibex, and Himalayan landscapes.

  • Overa-Aru Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Pahalgam, Anantnag district. Description: Rich in avian species and mammals, including the musk deer.

  • Hemis National Park Location: Ladakh region. Description: Famous for its snow leopards and high-altitude desert ecosystem.

  • Great Himalayan National Park Location: Kullu district. Description: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with rich biodiversity, including snow leopards, Himalayan brown bears, and rare birds.

  • Pin Valley National Park Location: Lahaul and Spiti district. Description: Known for its cold desert landscape and Himalayan wildlife.

  • Khirganga National Park Location: Kullu district. Description: Dense forest with a variety of flora and fauna, including Himalayan species.

  • Simbalbara National Park Location: Sirmaur district. Description: Forested park with leopards, barking deer, and vibrant birdlife.

  • Churdhar Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Sirmaur district. Description: Known for diverse vegetation and the Churdhar peak, a pilgrimage site.

  • Jim Corbett National Park Location: Nainital and Pauri Garhwal districts. Description: India’s oldest national park, famous for Bengal tigers, elephants, and dense Sal forests.

  • Rajaji National Park Location: Dehradun, Haridwar, and Pauri Garhwal districts. Description: Known for elephants, leopards, and scenic landscapes along the foothills of the Himalayas.

  • Valley of Flowers National Park Location: Chamoli district. Description: UNESCO World Heritage Site with alpine flowers, medicinal plants, and scenic valleys.

  • Nanda Devi National Park Location: Chamoli district. Description: Part of a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for rare wildlife and the Nanda Devi peak.

  • Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Almora district. Description: Rich in flora and fauna, with panoramic views of the Himalayan peaks.

  • Govind Wildlife Sanctuary and National Park Location: Uttarkashi district. Description: Home to snow leopards, black bears, and scenic valleys.

  • Gangotri National Park Location: Uttarkashi district. Description: Known for its glaciers, high-altitude wildlife, and Himalayan landscapes.

Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh & Rajasthan

  • Harike Wetland and Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Near the confluence of Beas and Sutlej rivers. Description: Known for migratory birds and rich wetland biodiversity.

  • Abohar Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Firozpur district. Description: Protects the blackbuck, considered a sacred animal by the local Bishnoi community.

  • Bir Moti Bagh Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Patiala district. Description: Rich in bird species and wooded grasslands

  • Kalesar National Park Location: Yamunanagar district. Description: Dense forest along the Shivalik Hills, home to leopards, elephants, and bird species.

  • Sultanpur National Park Location: Gurugram district. Description: A popular bird sanctuary known for migratory species, including flamingos and cranes.

  • Bir Shikargah Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Panchkula district. Description: Known for its forested areas and native wildlife.

  • Dudhwa National Park Location: Lakhimpur Kheri district. Description: Known for tigers, leopards, swamp deer, and dense grasslands.

  • Katarniaghat Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Bahraich district. Description: Known for Gangetic dolphins, tigers, and a wide variety of birds.

  • Chandra Prabha Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Chandauli district. Description: Features forests, waterfalls, and wildlife, including panthers and chinkaras.

  • Nawabganj Bird Sanctuary Location: Unnao district. Description: A wetland sanctuary known for a large number of migratory birds.

  • Sohagi Barwa Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Maharajganj district. Description: Rich in bird and animal species, including leopards and wild boars.

  • Sur Sarovar Bird Sanctuary (Keetham Lake) Location: Agra district. Description: Known for its large population of migratory birds and wetlands.

  • Hastinapur Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Across Meerut, Bijnor, Ghaziabad, and other districts. Description: Known for riverine and forest ecosystems with species like leopards and alligators.

  • Ranthambore National Park Location: Sawai Madhopur district. Description: Known for its large tiger population and ancient fort ruins.

  • Sariska Tiger Reserve Location: Alwar district. Description: A well-known tiger reserve with historical sites and diverse ecosystems.

  • Keoladeo National Park Location: Bharatpur district. Description: UNESCO World Heritage Site and a famous bird sanctuary, especially for migratory birds.

  • Desert National Park Location: Jaisalmer and Barmer districts. Description: Home to the Great Indian Bustard and unique desert flora and fauna.

  • Mount Abu Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Sirohi district. Description: Known for its cool climate, rich biodiversity, and rare plants.

  • Kumbhalgarh Wildlife Sanctuary Location: Rajsamand district. Description: Surrounds the Kumbhalgarh Fort, known for wolves and rich flora.

  • Tal Chhapar Sanctuary Location: Churu district. Description: Known for blackbucks and migratory birds like the harrier.
